What sits between them and you
A form was the answer. But when does that stop? And who positions themselves at the front when simple smart tech like this becomes the industry norm?

The guest who's curious about a Pelorus trip has spent the last hour talking to ChatGPT about it. Asking, refining, getting answers in seconds. Then they land on a website and meet a textarea labelled "tell us about your plans" and a "Schedule a Call" button. The next slot is seven days away.

The competition is no longer other travel companies. It's the standard the guest just experienced thirty seconds ago.

01
Trained on Pelorus's voice, philosophy and access
The AI carries the brand into every conversation. The way Pelorus talks, the way the team thinks, the places it can open. Not a generic assistant with a logo on top.
02
A real conversation, not a form
The guest gives detail because they want to, not because a textarea demands it. The asymmetry of value flips.
03
Always on. Any hour. Any timezone.
The 11pm Sunday enquiry. The 6am Singapore enquiry. The late-night-after-the-Svalbard-article enquiry. All met, properly, in the moment.
04
A hyper-personalised brief, delivered after every conversation
Who, when, what kind of landscape, what matters, budget, the specific moments they want engineered in. Delivered to the team before they pick up the phone.
05
Honest pushback when the numbers don't add up
Tight budgets, wrong season, mismatched destinations — the AI says so. A form can't do this. The single biggest signal of expertise.
06
Listens more than it speaks
Expert without ego. The visitor leaves feeling heard, not informed. Designed for the kind of person who doesn't need things explained.
07
Gets sharper the more it's used
Every conversation refines the next. The model learns the way Pelorus talks. Not the other way around.
08
Removes the friction between curiosity and commitment
No form. No scheduling link. No one-week wait. The moment a guest is curious — late at night, mid-conversation, after an article — there is something to engage with. Captured interest converts. Interest that had to wait, doesn't.
09
Positions Pelorus at the forefront of the industry
Every luxury travel brand will have some version of this within a few years. The ones who move now define what good looks like. The ones who wait inherit someone else's standard — and the comparison will not be flattering.
10
The team walks into every call already knowing the client
No cold open. No "tell me about yourself." The structured brief — who, when, where, what matters, budget, occasion — lands before the adviser picks up the phone. The first call becomes a second conversation. Deeper, faster, and far more likely to convert.
